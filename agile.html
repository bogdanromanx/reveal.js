Abstract Model

TEAM - Dimensions

  1. Process
  2. Team Composition
  3. Tools
  4. Communication
  5. Culture
  6. People
  1. Process, dealing with the methodical adoption of industry leading process/es that support the business and delivery needs with strong consideration for the environment in which they need to operate;
  2. Team Composition, deals with mobilisation of a high performance teams capable of working within the confines of the process, industry best practice and technologies;
  3. Tools, ensuring that the tools support the team and the process and compliment not hinder the productivity and collaboration;
  4. Communication, establishing relationships, agreeing timing, availability and mechanisms;
  5. Culture, identifying the differences in behaviour and expectation;
  6. People, deals with developing highly skilled personnel we need on projects, mentoring and training where appropriate;

TOP 5 Agile Gotchas

  1. Organisational willingness is essential
  2. Estimates will always set expectations
  3. Agile does not mean no requirements or only loosely defined requirements
  4. Agile projects still need governance
  5. Set reasonable expectations for induction, ramp-up and rotation

Agile is still fairly new to many organisations, especially the big ones.

Not all customers have the organisational culture to execute an Agile project.

Agile allows us to take a very high-level backlog and provide estimates.

We need well-defined requirements in order to deliver efficiently.

Be prepared to have the right people assigned to your projects to fulfil governance roles.
